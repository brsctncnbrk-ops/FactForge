## CTR Angle Explanation

The angle is reveal-driven: viewers arrive assuming the 2008 bailouts were a straightforward taxpayer-funded giveaway. The title and thumbnail promise the real accounting behind that headline number, and the video pays it off directly with TARP's actual net cost and AIG's profit — a genuine curiosity gap the story can honestly resolve. Human checklist note: every numeric claim above carries its fact ID in an HTML comment; "one fell, one didn't" (Lehman vs. AIG) is a framing choice (F003, F007) judged supportable — flagged here for the human reviewer per the Layer-2 checklist.

## Script Hook Promise

Main Viewer Promise:
One bank's collapse in September 2008 set off a chain reaction across the whole
financial system — and the rescue that followed cost far less than the number
everyone remembers, while the human cost landed on people the rescue never reached.

Hook Must Establish:
- A single, concrete collapse (Lehman Brothers) opens the story with real stakes
- The story is a step-by-step chain reaction, not a single villain's fraud
- The bailout numbers everyone remembers are not the numbers that actually happened

Avoid:
- Misleading angle (no "secret conspiracy" bait the video can't pay off)
- Slow generic intro ("In this video we will...")
- Political framing/blame language beyond what the sourced facts support
