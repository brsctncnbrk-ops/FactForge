## CTR Angle Explanation

The angle is contrarian-correction: viewers arrive "knowing" that barbarians destroyed Rome in 476. Title and thumbnail promise that this memory is wrong — the empire was already dead (internally, financially) and the famous moment was an anticlimax. That creates a strong curiosity gap ("then what actually killed it?") that the video genuinely pays off with the money/army/taxes chain, so the promise is clickable but honest. Human checklist note: every numeric claim above carries its fact ID in an HTML comment; the "payroll dispute" title is a framing of F022 (mutiny over refused land grants) — judged supportable, but it is a stylized simplification flagged here for the human reviewer.

## Script Hook Promise

Main Viewer Promise:
The fall of Rome was an inside job that took three hundred years — and the
famous "fall" of 476 barely mattered to the people living through it.

Hook Must Establish:
- Rome did not fall in one dramatic moment; almost nobody noticed 476
- The real killer was internal — money, army, and politics — not barbarian invasion
- The empire had been dying for three hundred years before the "end"

Avoid:
- Misleading angle (no "secret history" bait the video can't pay off)
- Slow generic intro ("In this video we will...")
- Unrelated background before the 476 cold open
